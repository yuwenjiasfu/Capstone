9. I think `oauth://fi.polar.Twitter_oAuth_Settings` is a custom scheme. Android custom scheme is not a globally unique security namespace. Other apps can declare the same scheme/host. If Twitter OAuth callback really depends on this scheme, scheme hijacking is possible. I think we should use Android App Links or explicit package name callback instead, and use state/PKCE in OAuth.

The modern Android signature system and Play distribution strategy may have mitigated some risks through other mechanisms; old signatures do not necessarily mean that they can currently be replaced and installed, because Android package signature verification also relies on certificate consistency; but 1024-bit RSA and SHA-1 It is not ideal for long-term security, especially the certificate validity period is very long; I think if it is still being maintained, it should be migrated to a stronger signature scheme and a modern APK Signature Scheme and confirm the upgrade path.
