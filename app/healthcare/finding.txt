Although `exported=false` itself reduces the direct attack surface, if any future sharing/opening file code passes the path that the attacker can influence to FileProvider, or the internal path verification fails, the scope of the URI grant will be much larger than actually needed; the evidence also includes `sources/com/yuwell/healthmanager/view/impl/Data.java` After exporting Excel, obtain the URI through FileProvider and use `ACTION_VIEW` and `sources/com/yuwell/healthmanager/view/impl/fetus/FhtResultActivity.java` to generate fetal heart rate result screenshots and then share the images through FileProvider; it is currently observed that the sharing objects mainly come from the cache/files path generated by the app itself. It is not confirmed that external Intent can directly control the FileProvider path, so it can only be used as a configuration risk and subsequent audit point.
